These are the indignities...

  • attempting a smile when my future mother-in-law
  • suggests a spa day, just for the ladies,
  • clutching my chest
  • after a loud noise in Chinatown,
  • praying my government contractor partner
  • will get to meet my grandfather,
  • intercepting at the restaurant
  • so my parents’ accents remain unspoken, undetected,
  • shrinking myself
  • as I press against the wall in the New York City subway,
  • pleading with my father to make his research less accessible,
  • less conspicuous, less interpretable,
  • clutching the armrest when the crowd descends,
  • ravenous and cruel, on the old kung-fu-teaching rat in the children’s movie,
  • swallowing a cough
  • to avoid drawing eyes in public,
  • listening to my mother describe, so casually,
  • how she can no longer bear to go to the supermarket,
  • and fear, and fear,
  • and fear.
